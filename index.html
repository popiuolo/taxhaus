Industries

Who we work with

We serve business owners across a range of industries, each with unique financial challenges. Select your industry to see how we can help.

Real Estate & Property

Landlords, investors, real estate professionals

Real estate businesses face complex tax rules and heightened CRA scrutiny — from GST/HST considerations to capital gains and ownership structures. Whether you own one property or a growing portfolio, proper reporting and long-term tax planning are critical to protecting your returns.

  • Rental income bookkeeping and reporting
  • Corporate tax planning for property ownership
  • GST/HST on new builds, flips, and commercial properties
  • Principal residence and capital gains planning
  • Property-specific expense tracking and CCA planning
  • Multi-property reporting and compliance
  • Long-term tax strategy for portfolio growth
  • Year-end tax filings (T2, T1)

Hospitality & Food Service

Restaurants, cafes, bars, food trucks, catering

Hospitality businesses operate on thin margins, high transaction volumes, and often seasonal cash flow. Accurate bookkeeping, sales reconciliation, and tax compliance are essential to understanding profitability and avoiding costly CRA issues.

  • Full-cycle bookkeeping and sales reconciliation
  • GST/HST filings and CRA compliance
  • Cost of goods sold (COGS) tracking
  • Cash-flow and margin analysis
  • Seasonal planning and budgeting
  • Year-end tax filings (T2, T1)

Retail & Consumer Businesses

Brick-and-mortar stores, pop-ups, product-based brands

Retail businesses require strong financial systems to manage inventory, sales tax, and cash flow. Between POS systems, inventory costs, and fluctuating demand, accurate reporting is critical for smart decision-making and long-term profitability.

  • Inventory-based bookkeeping
  • POS and sales tax reconciliation
  • GST/HST registration and filings
  • Profitability and pricing analysis
  • Cash-flow planning and forecasting
  • Year-end tax filings (T2, T1)

Creative & Media Professionals

Designers, photographers, videographers, influencers, artists

Creative professionals often deal with irregular income, project-based work, and blurred lines between personal and business expenses. Without proper planning, deductions are missed and tax surprises become common.

  • Tax planning for self-employed and incorporated creatives
  • Bookkeeping for project-based and irregular income
  • GST/HST compliance for creative services
  • Expense tracking for equipment and home office
  • Advisory for scaling creative businesses
  • Year-end tax filings (T2, T1)

Online & Digital Businesses

E-commerce, SaaS, creators, consultants, digital agencies

Online businesses move fast — but the finances behind them are rarely simple. Revenue flows through multiple platforms, payment processors, and sometimes multiple countries, while expenses are driven by software, advertising, and digital tools.

  • Corporate & personal tax planning for online income
  • E-commerce bookkeeping (Shopify, Stripe, PayPal, Amazon)
  • GST/HST registration and filings for digital sales
  • Cross-border tax considerations (US platforms, foreign customers)
  • Contractor vs employee structuring
  • Expense optimization for software, ads, and subscriptions
  • Year-end tax filings (T2, T1)

Construction, Trades & Skilled Labour

Contractors, builders, trades, subcontractors

Construction and trades businesses face complex reporting requirements — from job costing and subcontractors to payroll, WSIB, and GST/HST compliance. Cash flow is often seasonal, and CRA audits are common in this industry.

  • Job-costing and project-based bookkeeping
  • Payroll and subcontractor reporting (T4, T5018)
  • WSIB and CRA remittance support
  • GST/HST compliance for construction services
  • Vehicle and equipment deduction planning
  • Cash-flow and tax planning for seasonal income
  • Year-end tax filings (T2, T1)

Professional & Service-Based Businesses

Lawyers, consultants, coaches, agencies

Service-based businesses often generate strong income — but without proper structure and proactive planning, many professionals end up overpaying tax or dealing with avoidable compliance issues.

  • Incorporation & business structuring advice
  • Ongoing bookkeeping & financial reporting
  • Salary vs dividend planning
  • GST/HST compliance for services
  • Cash-flow and proactive tax planning
  • Year-end tax filings (T2, T1)

Healthcare & Medical

Doctors, dentists, clinics, physiotherapists, pharmacies

Healthcare professionals face unique tax considerations — from professional corporation structures and OHIP billing complexities to equipment depreciation and staff payroll. Proper planning can make a significant difference to your bottom line.

  • Professional corporation setup and management
  • Medical practice bookkeeping and reporting
  • Salary vs dividend optimization for practitioners
  • Equipment and leasehold improvement deductions
  • Payroll management for clinic staff
  • Year-end tax filings (T2, T1)
